About the Project

SoapLab is an educational web-based project developed as part of the Applied Mathematics â€“ Mathematical Modeling course. The project focuses on modeling chemical reactions involved in soap production and applying mathematical concepts to real-world chemical processes.

The website is designed to help students understand how chemical reactions, differential equations, and mathematical models are used in practical industrial applications such as soap manufacturing.

Project Objectives

The main objectives of this project are:

  • To explain the theory behind soap and its chemical composition
  • To model chemical reactions using differential equations
  • To apply mathematical modeling concepts to real-life soap production
  • To develop a simple calculator to estimate raw material quantities
  • To present scientific concepts in an interactive and user-friendly way

How the Project Works

The project is divided into several sections, each focusing on a specific aspect of soap production and chemical modeling.

The Theory section explains the basic concepts of soap, including its structure, types, and cleaning mechanism. The Chemical Reactions section introduces mathematical models of chemical reactions, including second-order reactions and radioactive decay, using differential equations.

The Calculator section allows users to input raw materials such as oils and alkali, and the system calculates the required quantities based on mathematical relationships.

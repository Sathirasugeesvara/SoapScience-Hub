Introduction to Chemical Reactions

A chemical reaction is a process of molecular rearrangement in which one or more substances, called reactants, are transformed into one or more new substances known as products. During this process, chemical bonds are broken and new bonds are formed.

In applied mathematics, chemical reactions are studied using differential equations to describe how the concentrations of substances change over time.

Rate of a Chemical Reaction

The rate of a chemical reaction refers to how fast the concentration of a reactant or product changes with respect to time.
